Led Zeppelin: An Overview

Led Zeppelin was an English rock band formed in London in 1968.

Along with Black Sabbath and Deep Purple, the band's heavy, guitar-driven sound has led them to be cited as one of the originators of heavy metal.

Their style drew from a wide variety of influences, including blues, psychedelic, and folk music.

Members

  • Robert Plant, Vocalist
  • Jimmy Page, Guitarist
  • John Paul Jones, Bassist/Keyboardist
  • John Bonham, Drummer
